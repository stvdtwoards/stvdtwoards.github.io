4D-Enhancer: Enhancing Spatio-temporal Consistency of 4D Content via Video Diffusion Priors

Abstract

4D content generation, encompassing dynamic 3D representations with temporal evolution, has emerged as a crucial technology for immersive applications. Existing approaches face fundamental challenges including coarse texture generation, poor surface quality, and spatiotemporal inconsistencies. Through systematic analysis, we identify a critical trade-off: 4D representation-based methods achieve superior spatial consistency but suffer from poor texture quality, while diffusion model-based approaches produce high-quality frames but struggle with spatiotemporal coherence. To address these limitations, we develop a specialized spatial-temporally consistent 4D Enhancer (4D-Enhancer) by extending video diffusion models to enhance the 4D results. Our enhancer is trained on a carefully curated dataset of 4D rendered videos paired with ground truth sequences. The enhancer demonstrates remarkable plug-and-play capability across various 4D generation methods, including 4D reconstruction, X-to-4D generation, and multi-view video generation, while significantly improving visual quality through its ability to maintain consistent high-fidelity textures and surface details. In this work, we show how the enhancer is efficiently integrated into the pipeline of these methods for generating 4D content, improving consistency and quality. Extensive experiments demonstrate that our method significantly outperforms existing state-of-the-art approaches in both quantitative metrics and qualitative assessments, while maintaining spatiotemporal consistency.
